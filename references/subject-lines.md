# Subject Lines

Reference for writing and testing cold email subject lines. Read this when the task is subjects specifically, or when finishing an email.

## Rules

- **Length:** aim for 3-6 words, under ~50 characters. Most inboxes truncate longer, and short reads as personal.
- **Lowercase or sentence case** usually outperforms Title Case (looks less like a broadcast).
- **No clickbait, no fake "Re:", no ALL CAPS, no emojis** in cold B2B. They trigger spam filters and burn trust.
- **Curiosity + relevance**, not hype. The subject earns the open; the first line earns the read.
- The subject should make sense next to the first line. Do not bait-and-switch.

## Patterns that work

1. **Question:** "worth a look?", "open to this?", "{{company}} + [outcome]?"
2. **Direct/plain:** "quick question", "idea for {{company}}", "[their role] at {{company}}"
3. **Observation:** "saw your Austin office", "your careers page"
4. **Outcome:** "2-3 more demos/week", "cut {{company}} bounce rate"
5. **Referral/context:** "{{first_name}}, [mutual context]"

Blank follow-up subject = reply in the same thread. Use this for later steps in a sequence so it reads as a continuation, not a new pitch.

## What to avoid (spam and trust risk)

- Money/urgency words: free, guarantee, act now, limited time, cash, $$$, winner.
- Excessive punctuation: "!!!", multiple "?".
- Misleading subjects (illegal under CAN-SPAM and a fast way to the spam folder).
- Personalization that will break: only use a merge field in the subject if the list has near-full coverage, with a fallback.

## Testing

- A/B test the subject AND the first line; they matter most for opens and replies.
- Judge on **real sends, not a hunch.** Need at least ~8-10 sends per variant before trusting a number, more for confidence.
- In GrowZen mode, read winners from `growzen_templates_list` with `sort=performance` and from `growzen_campaigns_analytics`. Do not declare a winner from a handful of sends.
- Change one thing at a time. If you swap subject and body together, you learn nothing.
